# Human Work Spectrum Classroom Kit

Instructor Guide

## Purpose

This kit helps students understand how AI changes the early-career bargain.

For decades, students were told that routine work would teach them how to become professionals. Read the memo. Build the spreadsheet. Format the deck. Summarize the case. Do enough lower-level work and eventually earn higher-level judgment.

AI is compressing that path.

The point of this session is not to make students afraid of AI. It is to help them name the kinds of human value that become more important when routine execution gets cheaper.

## Core Question

If AI takes over the grind, what kind of human value are you here to build?

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of the session, students should be able to:

1. Explain why AI changes entry-level work and professional development.
2. Describe the 4I Framework: interpretive, integrative, interpersonal, imaginative.
3. Identify their top Human Work Spectrum archetypes.
4. Translate their archetype result into a 30-day development plan.
5. Name one boundary for how they should and should not use AI in their work.

## Recommended Prep

Before class, ask students to:

1. Take the assessment at https://humanworkspectrum.com/quiz.html
2. Save or screenshot their top three archetypes.
3. Open their My Spectrum dashboard at https://humanworkspectrum.com/app
4. Read the short student pre-work handout.

## Session Options

Use one of three formats:

- 45-minute lecture: best for guest talks or quick module insertion.
- 75-minute class plan: best for normal class sessions.
- 90-minute workshop: best for career centers, capstones, or executive education.

## Facilitation Notes

Do not let the session become a generic AI tools discussion. Students already know AI can produce text, slides, summaries, and code. The deeper issue is professional identity.

Keep returning to this distinction:

- AI changes what tasks cost.
- Human Work Spectrum asks what human value is still scarce.

The best discussion usually starts when students realize their top archetype is not a personality label. It is a development path.

## Suggested Grading

For a graded assignment, use the Post-Grind Value Plan.

Suggested rubric:

- 30% understanding of the 4I Framework and archetypes.
- 30% specificity of the student's development plan.
- 20% quality of AI boundary.
- 20% reflection on how their career assumptions need to change.

## Instructor Close

End with this:

AI may remove parts of the work students expected to do first. That does not remove their future value. It raises the bar for naming, practicing, and proving that value earlier.
