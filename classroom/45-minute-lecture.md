# 45-Minute Lecture

Title: After the Grind: What Humans Do When AI Does the Work

## Lecture Goal

Introduce the problem, the 4I Framework, and the Human Work Spectrum archetypes quickly enough that students can connect the ideas to their own careers.

## Timing

### 0-5 minutes: Opening Question

Ask:

What part of your first professional job do you think AI will be able to do before you get there?

Let students answer quickly. Write examples on the board:

- Summaries
- Reports
- Presentations
- Market research
- Data cleaning
- Draft emails
- Basic analysis

Then say:

That list is not just a task list. It used to be the training ground.

### 5-12 minutes: The Old Bargain

Explain:

The old career bargain was simple. You did the grind first. The grind was repetitive, lower-status, and sometimes boring, but it taught you how work worked.

AI changes the bargain because it can do much of the grind faster, cheaper, and at acceptable quality.

Key point:

The problem is not that humans have no value. The problem is that the entry point moved.

### 12-22 minutes: The 4I Framework

Introduce the four domains of human value:

1. Interpretive: making meaning from information.
2. Integrative: connecting parts into functioning wholes.
3. Interpersonal: building trust, judgment, and human capacity.
4. Imaginative: seeing futures, possibilities, risks, and meanings not yet obvious.

Prompt:

Which of these do you think your major trains most directly? Which one does it undertrain?

### 22-32 minutes: The 10 Archetypes

Frame archetypes as development paths, not personality boxes.

Interpretive:
- Translator
- Sensemaker
- Signal Architect

Integrative:
- Bridge Builder
- Architect
- Orchestrator

Interpersonal:
- Liaison
- Mentor of Mentors
- Narrative Carrier

Imaginative:
- Reflective Futurist

Ask:

Which one sounds most valuable in a team where AI is already doing routine work?

### 32-40 minutes: Student Reflection

Ask students to open their assessment result.

Individual writing:

1. My primary archetype is:
2. This means I probably notice:
3. This means I can help teams:
4. One skill I need to build is:
5. One AI boundary I need is:

### 40-45 minutes: Close

Close with:

Your career will not be built by proving you can do tasks a machine can already do. It will be built by showing the judgment, trust, integration, imagination, and human responsibility that machines cannot carry for you.

Assignment:

Complete the Post-Grind Value Plan.
